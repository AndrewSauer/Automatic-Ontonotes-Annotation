Ljiljana Raičević (; born 29 June 1947 , née Petrović ) is a human rights and women 's rights activist in Serbia and Montenegro . 

She was the 2006 laureate of Amnesty International 's Ginetta Sagan Fund Award . 

Raicevic created Montenegro 's SOS LINE , the first NGO that in that country which served women by providing resources for women in abusive relationships . 

After noticing that the women calling the SOS LINE needed additional assistance , such a safe place to stay , as well as medical , psychological , and legal assistance , Raičević established the Women 's Safe House , the first shelter in Montenegro for victims of domestic violence . 

With the Safe House , she successfully lobbied the Parliament of Montenegro for the adoption of a witness protection law .
