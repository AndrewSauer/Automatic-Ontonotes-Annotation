Han Lei (; born February 23 , 1968 ) , also known in Mongolian as Senbor , is a Chinese folk and pop singer - songwriter . 

Han was born and raised in Hohhot , Inner Mongolia . 

He is of Mongol descent through his mother , and Han Chinese descent through his father . 

Han rose to prominence in 1997 on the CCTV New Year 's Gala , performing a song called " 1997 , A.D. " ( 公元一九九七 ) ; in 1998 he returned to the Gala to perform " Zou Sifang " ( 走四方 ) , one of the most memorable performances in the song and dance category of the Gala that year , which became his signature hit and propelled him to national stardom . 

He has since appeared on the Gala six more times , the most recent performance in the 2014 show . 

Han has also recorded introductory and conclusion theme songs for various Chinese TV Series , including , perhaps most famously , the introductory theme to Kangxi Dynasty and The Emperor in Han Dynasty , " Xiang Tian Zai Jie Wu Bai Nian " ( 向天在借五百年 ) . 

He also performed the main theme " I 'll Go with you to the Ends of the Earth " ( 跟着你到天边 ) in the Zhang Yimou film Coming Home . 

Han also participated and was the winner of the Hunan Television show I Am a Singer ( season 2 ) .
