The Middle Fork John Day River is a tributary of the North Fork John Day River in the U.S. state of Oregon . 

It originates in the Blue Mountains of northeast Oregon in the Malheur National Forest near Austin and flows generally west to the North Fork about above Monument . 

The Middle Fork drainage basin covers about . 

The Oregon Scenic Waterways Program , administered by the Oregon Parks and Recreation Department ( OPRD ) protects the river for most of its length . 

The state lists a segment of the Middle Fork from about from the mouth to about from the mouth as a Scenic River Area and the lower as a Natural River Area . 

People planning to cut trees , mine , build roads or structures , or to make other substantial changes within of the river must first notify OPRD and seek its approval .
