Athetis hospes , or Porter 's rustic , is a moth of the family Noctuidae which was described by Christian Friedrich Freyer in 1835 . 

It is found in Spain , southern France , Italy , on the Balkan Peninsula , Crete , Turkey and northern Iran . 

The species seems to be expanding its range in north - western Europe with records from Great Britain and the Netherlands . 

The wingspan is 26–30   mm . 

Adults are on wing from May to June and again from August to September . 

The larvae feed on various low - growing plants , including tobacco . 

The species overwinters as full - grown larvae in the soil . 

They are gregarious in the first instar . 

Later instars make galleries in the stalks and holes in the leaves . 

Pupation takes place below ground near the host plant
