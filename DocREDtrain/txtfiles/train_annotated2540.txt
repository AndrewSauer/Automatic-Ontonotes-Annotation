Padma Bahadur Khatri ( 19 July 1915 - 1985 ) was a former foreign minister of Nepal , and also served as an army officer and diplomat . 

He began his career as a soldier , and eventually attained the rank of Major General . 

He delivered Nepal 's first application for membership of the United Nations in 1948 , which was eventually accepted in 1955 . 

He served as Nepal 's Permanent Representative to the United Nations , and was twice the President of the Security Council when Nepal was a member of the Council in the 1970s . 

In 1962 he was chairman of the Nepal - China Boundary Commission that successfully demarcated the Nepalese - Tibetan border . 

He served twice as the Nepali Ambassador to the United States in the 1960s and 1970s . 

In 1982 , he was appointed as foreign minister by the King of Nepal . 

At the time of his death , he was a member of the National Assembly of Nepal .
