Pope John XXIII ( 25 November 1881 – 3 June 1963 ) and Pope John Paul II ( 18 May 1920 – 2 April 2005 ) reigned as popes of the Roman Catholic Church and the sovereigns of Vatican City ( respectively from 1958 to 1963 and 1978 to 2005 ) . 

Their canonizations were held on 27 April 2014 . 

The decision to canonize was made official by Pope Francis on 5 July 2013 following the recognition of a miracle attributed to the intercession of John Paul II , while John XXIII was canonized for his merits of opening the Second Vatican Council . 

The date of the canonization was assigned on 30 September 2013 . 

The Canonization Mass was celebrated by Pope Francis ( with Pope Emeritus Benedict XVI concelebrating ) , on 27 April 2014 ( Divine Mercy Sunday ) , in St. Peter 's Square ( Pope John Paul had died on the vigil of Divine Mercy Sunday in 2005 ) . 

About 150 Cardinals and 700 bishops concelebrated the Mass , and at least 500,000 people attended the Mass with an estimated 300,000 others watching from video screens placed around Rome .
