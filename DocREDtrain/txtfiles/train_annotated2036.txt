Sheila Cecelia Escovedo ( born December 12 , 1957 ) better known under the stage name Sheila E. , is an American percussionist , singer , author , and actress . 

She began her career in the mid 1970s as a percussionist and singer for The George Duke Band . 

After leaving the group in 1983 , Sheila began a successful solo career , starting with her critically acclaimed debut album , which included the career - defining song , " The Glamorous Life " . 

She became a mainstream solo star in 1985 following the success of the singles , " The Belle of St. Mark " , " Sister Fate " , and " A Love Bizarre " with the last becoming one of her signature songs . 

She is commonly referred to as The Queen of Percussion .
