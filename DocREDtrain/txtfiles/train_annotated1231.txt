Dianne Jackson ( July 28 , 1941 â€“ December 31 , 1992 ) was an English animation director , best known for The Snowman , made in 1982 and subsequently repeated every Christmas on Channel 4 in the United Kingdom . 

She had a long career as an animator , and her earliest credit was for The Beatles ' Yellow Submarine in 1968 . 

She is particularly noted for recreating the style of the original artists in her animations , for example of Raymond Briggs 's picture book , The Snowman . 

She also directed Granpa by John Burningham in 1989 and was due to direct Raymond Briggs ' Father Christmas in 1991 , having completed storyboarding for the film , although due to her illness this was directed by Dave Unwin . 

She also planned the first series of animated adaptations of the tales of Beatrix Potter as The World of Peter Rabbit and Friends . 

However , she died of cancer on New Year 's Eve 1992 at the age of 51 . 

The series episode The Tailor of Gloucester is dedicated to her memory .
