Henry Dalziel , VC ( 18 February 1893 â€“ 24 July 1965 ) was an Australian recipient of the Victoria Cross ( VC ) , the highest award for gallantry in the face of the enemy that can be awarded to British and Commonwealth forces . 

He was awarded the VC while serving with the Australian Imperial Force during the First World War . 

Dalziel 's VC was the 1,000th awarded . 

After the war , Dalziel returned to Australia and tried to make a living by farming . 

Troubled by the injuries he sustained during the war , he left the land and took up factory work . 

He moved between jobs several times during the 1930s , and led something of a transient lifestyle , even at one stage turning to gold prospecting . 

In the mid-1930s he rejoined the army in a part - time capacity and during the Second World War served in a training role in Australia . 

He died in 1965 at the age of 72 .
