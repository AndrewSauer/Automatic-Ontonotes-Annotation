David Doubilet ( born November 28 , 1946 ) is a well known underwater photographer known primarily for his work published in National Geographic Magazine where he is a contributing photographer and has been an author for 70 feature articles since 1971 . 

He was born in New York City and started taking photos underwater at the young age of 12 . 

He started with a Brownie Hawkeye in a rubber anesthesiologist 's bag to keep the water out of the camera . 

He lived with his family in New York City and spent summers in Elberon New Jersey exploring the Atlantic . 

He later worked as a diver and photographer for the Sandy Hook Marine Laboratories in New Jersey and spent much of his youth in the Caribbean as a teenage dive instructor in the Bahamas where he found his motivation to capture the beauty of the sea and everything in it .
