Irene May Guest ( July 22 , 1900 – June 14 , 1970 ) , also known by her married name Irene Loog , was an American competition swimmer , Olympic champion , and world record - holder . 

She represented the United States as a 19-year - old at the 1920 Summer Olympics in Antwerp , Belgium , where she received a pair of medals . 

Guest received her first medal in the women 's 100-meter freestyle in which she finished second behind fellow American Ethelda Bleibtrey , earning a silver medal with a time of 1:17.0 . 

In the women 's 4×100 metres freestyle relay , she won a gold medal with U.S. teammates Bleibtrey , Frances Schroth and Margaret Woodbridge in a new world record time of 5:11.6 . 

Guest was inducted into the International Swimming Hall of Fame as an " honor pioneer swimmer " in 1990 .
