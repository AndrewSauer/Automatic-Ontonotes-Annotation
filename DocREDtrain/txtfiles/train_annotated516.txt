The Head of Janus ( ) is a 1920 German horror silent film directed by F. W. Murnau . 

The film was an unauthorized adaptation of Robert Louis Stevenson 's novel The Strange Case of Dr. Jekyll and Mr. Hyde , but the source material went unrecognized by some of the German media due to changes in the characters ' names . 

Released on 17 September 1920 by the Lipow Co. , this is one of Murnau 's lost films . 

The screenplay was written by Hans Janowitz , who collaborated with Carl Mayer on the script for The Cabinet of Dr. Caligari ( 1919 ) . 

While the film itself does not survive , the scripts and related production notes do . 

Because the film is lost , its full length is unknown .
