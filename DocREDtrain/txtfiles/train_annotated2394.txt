Edith L. Tiempo ( April 22 , 1919 â€“ August 21 , 2011 ) , poet , fiction writer , teacher and literary critic was a Filipino writer in the English language . 

Tiempo was born in Quezon City , but later became a resident of Kawit , Cavite . 

Her poems are intricate verbal transfigurations of significant experiences as revealed , in two of her much anthologized pieces , " Halaman " and " Bonsai . " 

As fictionist , Tiempo is as morally profound . 

Her language has been marked as " descriptive but unburdened by scrupulous detailing . " 

She is an influential tradition in Philippine Literature in English . 

Together with her late husband , writer and critic Edilberto K. Tiempo , they founded ( in 1962 ) and directed the Silliman National Writers Workshop in Dumaguete City , which has produced some of the Philippines ' best writers . 

She was conferred the National Artist Award for Literature in 1999 .
