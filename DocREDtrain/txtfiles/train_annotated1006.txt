Tikhon Nikolayevich Khrennikov (;   – 14 August 2007 ) , HSL , PAU , was a Russian and Soviet composer , pianist , and leader of the Union of Soviet Composers , who was also known for his political activities . 

He wrote three symphonies , four piano concertos , two violin concertos , two cello concertos , operas , operettas , ballets , chamber music , incidental music and film music . 

During the 1930s , Khrennikov was already being hailed as a leading official Soviet composer . 

In 1948 , Andrei Zhdanov , the leader of the anti - formalism campaign , nominated Khrennikov as Secretary of the Union of Soviet Composers . 

He held this influential post until the collapse of the Soviet Union in 1991 .
