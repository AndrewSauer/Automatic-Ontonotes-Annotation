Dorothy Ray Healey ( September 22 , 1914 â€“ August 6 , 2006 ) was a long - time activist in the Communist Party USA , from the late 1920s to the 1970s . 

In the 1930s , she was one of the first union leaders to advocate for the rights of Chicanos and blacks as factory and field workers . 

During the decades of the 1950s and 1960s , Healey was one of the leading public figures of the Communist Party in the state of California . 

An opponent of the Soviet invasion of Czechoslovakia in 1968 and at odds with the orthodox pro - Soviet leadership of Gus Hall , Healey subsequently left the Communist Party to join the New American Movement , which merged to become part of the Democratic Socialists of America in 1982 .
