The Azerbaijani Popular Front Party ( APFP ) ( , ) is an opposition political party in Azerbaijan , founded in 1992 by Abulfaz Elchibey . 

After Elchibey 's death in 2000 , the party split into two factions , the reform wing led by Ali Kerimli and the classical wing led by Mirmahmud Miralioglu . 

During 5 November 2000 ( and 7 January 2001 ) parliamentary elections , the party won 11.0 % of the popular vote and 6 out of 125 seats in the National Assembly of Azerbaijan . 

Its candidate Gudrat Hasanguliyev won only 0.4 % of the popular vote in the 15 October 2003 presidential elections . 

At the parliamentary elections of 6 November 2005 , APF joined the Freedom ( ) block but won only one seat .
