Polish – Ukrainian relations as international relations were revived soon after Ukraine gained independence from the Soviet Union in the early 1990s . 

They have been both improving and deteriorating since . 

Various controversies from their shared history occasionally resurface in Polish – Ukrainian relations , but they are not having a major influence on the bilateral relations of Poland and Ukraine . 

Ukraine and Poland are respectively , the second and third largest Slavic countries , after Russia . 

The two countries share a border of about 529   km . 

Poland 's acceptance of the Schengen Agreement created problems with the Ukrainian border traffic . 

On July 1 , 2009 , an agreement on local border traffic between the two countries came into effect , which enables Ukrainian citizens living in border regions to cross the Polish frontier according to a liberalized procedure . 

Ukraine is the country with the largest number of Polish consulates .
